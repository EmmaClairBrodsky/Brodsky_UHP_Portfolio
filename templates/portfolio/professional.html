Research Intern · May–Dec 2025

Procedural Texturing Using Triplanar Mapping

NC State College of Engineering & College of Textiles

May 2025 through December 2025, I had the opportunity to serve as a Research Intern for a Procedural Texturing project through both the College of Engineering and the College of Textiles. This work allowed me to gain experience in using both software tools and industry-standard design tools, as I would need to do as a software engineer for animation pipelines. Using the Maya API and Python PIL library, my professor and I worked closely with textile students to develop a dynamic texture adjustment system that would allow designers to efficiently model clothing that accommodates diverse body types and mobility needs. Through conducting research, collaborating with artists, and working on projects, I have learned that software and art are intricately interconnected and are a driving force for positive change.

Campus Involvement
Aug 2023–Present

NCSU Engineering Ambassador

August 2023, I was admitted to the NCSU Engineering Ambassadors program. The Engineering Ambassador team is a select group of NCSU engineering students who host both on-campus and off-campus engineering events for current students, prospective students, and K-12 students. I have had the incredible opportunity of participating in both prospective student outreach and K-12 outreach. I am passionate about being an Engineering Ambassador because it allows me to speak personably with prospective students who have the same fears I had when I was beginning to apply to undergraduate programs: that I would have to choose between my interests. Through my time at NCSU, I've learned how to balance and navigate my interdisciplinary course load, and I enjoy sharing my wisdom and potential academic paths with incoming students.

Project Experience
Jan–May 2025 · CSC 326

WolfCafe — Professional Full-Stack Project

January 2025 through May 2025, I was enrolled in CSC 326: Software Engineering course. The goal of this course is to simulate a professional environment, including a software engineering team, progress reports, and a culminating, deployable final assignment. My team worked on an application called WolfCafe that simulated an online cafe ordering system. There were roles for admin (to manage staff), staff (to manage inventory, menu items, and to view and fulfill orders), and customers (to order menu items). Through this project, I was able to gain experience in both a backend and frontend software engineering role and in software engineering practices currently used in industry.
