Academic Portfolio

Interdisciplinary learning across Computer Science and Art & Design.

High-Impact Experience

Disciplinary Breadth

Through my Computer Science major in the College of Engineering and my Art & Design minor through the College of Design, I have exhibited a commitment to interdisciplinary learning and growth. My unique undergraduate courseload helped me discover that I am passionate about creating impactful and exciting experiences through the medium of animation. Though balancing my coursework and refining my academic path was initially difficult, I eventually found a focus in computer graphics. Now, because of my initial struggle, I can step into a mentor role, and help prospective students who are also seeking to combine their interests in art and technology. Below are several artifacts from experiences during my interdisciplinary education.

Computer Science Coursework
Fall 2025 · CSC 461

Computer Graphics

In Fall 2025 I took CSC 461: Computer Graphics. Through this course, I learned about the fundamentals of graphics programming and creating custom shaders. For our final project, we recreated the iconic arcade game BattleZone, but built the renderer from the ground up. This allowed us to take full control of the way our game looked and performed. I took this class after researching computer graphics as an industry I would potentially be interested in pursuing, and learned that I thrive in environments that allow me to think both technically and creatively.

Spring 2025 · CSC 486

Computational Visual Narrative

In Spring 2025, I took CSC 486: Computational Visual Narrative. At the beginning of the semester, teams selected a single narrative that they would develop across four games created over the duration of the course. The goal was to convey a consistent story both visually and through game mechanics, regardless of genre. This course taught me how to intentionally leverage the medium I am working in to construct narrative, and reinforced that user experience drives a story. I can keep this fact in mind as I design tools for creatives in a technical director role: the software I create influences the medium, and therefore the way artists can share their stories. Through this course, I learned to be mindful of my end users when designing my software tools, and how to take advantage of the tools I am given to convey my stories meaningfully.

Art & Design Coursework
Fall 2023 · ADN 112

3-D Design

Fall 2023, I took my first class through my Art & Design minor, 3-D Design. Through this course I worked with OnShape, a CADing software commonly used in engineering disciplines and Blender, which is a free, open-source 3-D modeling program. The class allowed me to practice using 3-D tools as a beginner and start applying the principles of design to my 3-D works. I am most proud of my final project in this class: a garden-themed chess set. I had to think critically about how I wanted each piece to be represented and was able to practice modeling, lighting, and texturing. Through this course, I learned that I love working with 3-D software, which inspired me to continue taking classes centered on modeling throughout my Art & Design minor and even through my Engineering electives.

Spring 2025 · ADN 319

Animation I

Spring 2025, I had the opportunity to take my first formal animation class through my Art & Design minor. Through this class, I was able to use the industry-standard animation program, Maya, and gain experience in timing, acting, motion, and animation best practices. My professor was incredibly supportive of my interests in both technology and art, and introduced me to MEL scripting, which is the programming language used to create tools and shortcuts for rigs in Maya. Technical directors use MEL scripting frequently, and learning this skill was invaluable to my career goal of becoming a technical director and working between artists and engineers.

UHP & Storytelling Coursework
Spring 2024 · HON 202

Graphic Novels: Text, Image, Interpretation

In Spring 2024, I took an Honors Inquiry, Discovery, and Literature class specializing in graphic novels. This class allowed me to learn and practice visual storytelling, as well as introduced me to biases and stereotypes perpetuated by the comic industry. These stereotypes often persist in other mediums of art, such as animation, even presently, due to the popularity of comics. Learning about the role visual storytelling has played in both challenging and persisting harmful stereotypes allowed me to become a more mindful creator myself. For my final project, I created a superhero character design that actively challenged stereotypes of hypersexual femininity in superhero comics. This exercise was important to me, in-particular, as a woman in STEM, because it allowed me to think critically about how women are portrayed in media, how those portrayals can influence who feels welcome in technical fields, and how I can alter my own work to empower others.

Spring 2025 · ENG 288

Fiction Writing

In Spring 2025, I took ENG 288: Fiction writing as a requirement for the CSC game development concentration. Though I decided game development was not for me, I did enjoy taking this course to build on my time in the Creative Writing Crew and improve my writing and storytelling skills. I chose to write about a story based on my time at my Catholic High School, which allowed me to better understand how strong storytelling supports meaningful visual and interactive experiences. This allowed me to reflect on my own experiences while practicing character development, dialogue, and structured storytelling.
